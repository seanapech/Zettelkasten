####  Chapter 1, Principles | 1.1, Simplicity

	"Arch Linux defines simplicity as without unnecessary additions or modifications. It ships software as released by the original developers--upstream--with minimal distrubution-specific downstream changes."

Arch Linux is designed with the intention of serving the end-user, not the average user. Contrary to popular belief, Arch also strives to be stable via continuity; This means that all shipped software--i.e. system update/upgrade by user with the ``sudo pacman -Syu`` command--is sourced from the original developers and their repository, and avoid prescribing patches or bug fixes that are distribution specific. In other words, any updates or patches from the developers will have been made to serve any machine running Arch Linux, and not just machines running specific forks or versions of Arch Linux.
